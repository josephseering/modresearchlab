Online Community Chatbot Study

Human-Computer Interaction Institute, Carnegie Mellon University

Baby Bot is an interactive chatbot developed by researchers at Carnegie Mellon University. Its purpose is to test possibilities for new chatbot features that allow them to integrate better with online communities. Baby Bot will be deployed for three weeks in selected Twitch communities, from June 1st 2019 to June 19th 2019.

Features and themes

Baby Bot is designed to "grow up" and mature over time, moving from its initial state as a baby through childhood, adolescence, and finally to adulthood. As it grows up, it will more about its home communities and will begin to converse in more nuanced ways. As an infant, it seeks care and attention, but in later phases of its growth it will ask questions and talk with viewers in ways that it has learned from listening to conversations.

Data and privacy

Like a human child, Baby Bot needs to listen to and remember what people around it have said in order to learn how to talk. In order to do this, its algorithms see and record public messages sent to its home channels. These messages are saved in a standard spreadsheet, which its algorithms use to create sentences for it to say as it grows up. Baby Bot does not and cannot see private messages (whispers) and does not record any information other than what is visible to everyone in the chat. Use of algorithms

Baby Bot's text generation algorithms are based in part on machine learning algorithms, which means that what it says is not always scripted. There are safeguards in place to help ensure that Baby Bot does not use offensive or hateful language, but it is possible that it may say things that are annoying or unintentionally unpleasant. The research team will be actively monitoring Baby Bot's behavior for the entire duration of the study, and may temporarily disable certain features if it seems that positive standards for behavior are not being met. Note that, as is the case with any user on Twitch, you may also block Baby Bot if you find it unpleasant.
